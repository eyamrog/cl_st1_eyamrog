The following extracts exemplify the excessive reliance on cued elicitation (Mercer, 1995) and Socratic questioning (Roth, 1996), which serve to limit discourse to a narrow line of reasoning. In this context, students were required to provide brief responses that aligned with the scientific explanation being presented. In the first instance, the lesson aimed to compare the evaporation of water and perfume; however, as students were unable to observe or perceive the evaporation of water directly, the perfume was utilized to illustrate the concept of a substance transitioning from liquid to gas and dispersing into the air. The teacher elaborated on this reasoning in Extract 2.
